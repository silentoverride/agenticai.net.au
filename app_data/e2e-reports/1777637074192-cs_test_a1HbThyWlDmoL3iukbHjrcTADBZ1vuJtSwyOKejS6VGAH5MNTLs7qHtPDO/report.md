## Executive Summary
Harbour Tours spends too much owner time on enquiries, scheduling, quote follow-up, and review requests. The best opportunities are low-risk automation around inbox triage, booking coordination, and follow-up reporting.

## Pain Points
- **Manual enquiry triage** (high, daily): Tour enquiries arrive by email and phone and are manually copied into a spreadsheet before follow-up.
- **Slow quote follow-up** (medium, weekly): The owner personally checks quote status and sends reminders.

## Quick Wins
- **Shared inbox triage assistant** — Classify enquiries, draft replies, and extract requested dates and group size for staff review.
  - Effort: low, Impact: high, ~5 hrs/week saved
- **Post-tour review follow-up** — Send templated review requests and flag unhappy customers for manual follow-up.
  - Effort: low, Impact: medium, ~2 hrs/week saved

## Deeper Opportunities
- **Booking workflow hub** — Move spreadsheet-based scheduling into a single booking operations table with automated status changes.
  - Setup cost: $3500 AUD, Monthly value: $2600 AUD

## Recommended Tools
- **Airtable** (workflow database) — Track enquiries, bookings, quote status, and customer follow-up in one operational view.
  - ~$80/month | Setup: medium
- **Zapier** (automation) — Connect Gmail, forms, booking records, and reminder messages.
  - ~$45/month | Setup: low

## Implementation Roadmap
### Stabilise enquiry capture (1-2)
- Create standard enquiry fields
- Route new enquiries into a booking table
- Prepare reply templates for staff approval

### Automate follow-up (3-4)
- Add quote reminder rules
- Add review request workflow
- Create weekly owner summary

### Improve operations visibility (5-8)
- Dashboard bookings by status
- Review bottlenecks and missed follow-ups
- Tune automations from staff feedback
