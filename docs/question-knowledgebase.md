# AI Business Assessment Question Knowledgebase

This knowledgebase supports the AI Business Assessment intake agent. Start with the general questions, then select one or two industry modules that match the caller's business. Do not ask every question. The agent should choose the most relevant questions based on the caller's answers.

## Selection Rules

- Ask all critical general discovery questions unless the answer has already been provided.
- Select industry questions only after the business type is clear.
- Ask no more than 20 to 30 minutes of questions in one intake call.
- Prioritise workflows with clear frequency, owner involvement, customer impact, or revenue impact.
- Ask for examples whenever a caller uses vague phrases like "admin", "follow-up", "manual", "messy", "spreadsheet", or "chasing".
- Do not prescribe tools during intake. Capture the need and context.

## General Discovery Questions

### Business Model

- What is your name and role in the business?
- What is the company or business name?
- What is the best email address for assessment updates and the report?
- What is the best phone number to contact you if the assessment team needs to clarify anything?
- What does the business sell?
- Who are your best customers or clients?
- How does the business make money?
- What is the average value of a new customer, project, case, booking, or sale?
- Is the business mostly one-off transactions, recurring clients, retainers, projects, memberships, or appointments?
- What are the busiest periods of the week, month, or year?

### Team and Roles

- How many people are involved in day-to-day operations?
- Which roles exist on the team?
- Which tasks are handled by the owner?
- Which tasks are handled by admin, sales, operations, finance, or external contractors?
- Where does the team rely on one person's knowledge?
- Where do things slow down if one person is unavailable?

### Tools and Data

- What software tools do you use every day?
- What do you use for CRM or customer records?
- What do you use for bookings, jobs, projects, or case tracking?
- What do you use for accounting, invoicing, payments, or payroll?
- What do you use for internal communication?
- What do you use for documents, files, or knowledge storage?
- Which tools do not talk to each other?
- Where are spreadsheets still used as the source of truth?

### Pain Points

- What is the biggest headache in your workday right now?
- What task do you repeat even though it feels like it should be automated?
- What does the team complain about most often?
- What customer issue keeps coming back?
- What work is important but often delayed?
- Which workflow has too many steps?
- Which process creates the most rework?
- Which part of the business feels hardest to scale?

### Lead and Customer Response

- Where do new enquiries come from?
- What happens after an enquiry arrives?
- How quickly do you respond?
- Who qualifies the enquiry?
- What questions do prospects ask before buying or booking?
- How many leads are missed or delayed in a typical week?
- What causes leads to fall through the cracks?
- What would one additional qualified lead, booking, or sale per month be worth?

### Operations and Admin

- What reports, emails, documents, quotes, or updates are created manually?
- What information is copied between systems?
- What checklists or recurring processes happen every week?
- Which tasks require someone to search through emails, files, or messages?
- Where are approvals needed?
- Which handoffs create delays?
- What work happens outside your main software because the system cannot handle it?

### Knowledge and Training

- Where are standard operating procedures documented?
- How do new staff learn the business?
- What questions does the team ask repeatedly?
- What customer questions are answered repeatedly?
- What knowledge is trapped in the owner's head?
- Which documents would be useful for an AI assistant to search or explain?
- How often do procedures, pricing, products, or policies change?

### Reporting and Decisions

- What numbers do you need to see regularly?
- How are those numbers collected?
- Who prepares reports?
- How long does reporting take?
- Which decisions are made from incomplete information?
- What dashboard or report would be valuable if it updated automatically?
- Where do you discover problems too late?

### Constraints

- What customer or business data is sensitive?
- Are there compliance rules, privacy obligations, or industry standards we should know about?
- Which systems cannot be changed?
- What budget range is realistic for implementation if the assessment finds a strong opportunity?
- Who approves new software or workflow changes?
- What timeline would make sense for a first quick win?

## Industry Modules

## Professional Services

Use for consultants, agencies, accountants, bookkeepers, lawyers, architects, engineers, brokers, and advisory firms.

- How do new clients first contact the firm?
- What information do you need before deciding whether a client is a fit?
- How are proposals, scopes, engagement letters, or quotes created?
- What parts of client onboarding are repeated?
- How do you collect documents or information from clients?
- What questions do clients ask repeatedly during delivery?
- Where does work get stuck waiting for client information?
- How do you track project, matter, job, or engagement status?
- How do you record important client decisions or advice?
- Which reports or updates do clients expect regularly?
- What knowledge is stored in previous client work that would be useful to reuse?
- Where does senior staff time get used on work a junior person or assistant could handle?

## Real Estate and Property

Use for real estate agencies, buyers agents, property managers, mortgage brokers, conveyancers, developers, and property services.

- How do new buyer, seller, tenant, landlord, or borrower enquiries arrive?
- How quickly are enquiries followed up?
- What questions do prospects ask before booking an appraisal, inspection, consult, or call?
- How are leads qualified?
- How are property details, listing information, or borrower details collected?
- Which documents are repeatedly requested or explained?
- How are inspections, appointments, or open homes scheduled?
- How do you follow up after an inspection or enquiry?
- What updates do sellers, landlords, buyers, tenants, or borrowers expect?
- What reporting is prepared manually for owners, vendors, lenders, or management?
- Which compliance or disclosure steps are easy to miss?
- Where does deal or listing knowledge live today?

## Healthcare, Allied Health, and Wellness

Use for clinics, dental practices, allied health, psychology, NDIS providers, fitness studios, and wellness businesses. Do not ask for personal health details.

- How do new patient, client, or participant enquiries arrive?
- How are appointments booked, confirmed, rescheduled, or cancelled?
- What intake forms or eligibility checks are required?
- What questions do people ask before booking?
- How are reminders handled?
- Where do no-shows or late cancellations create cost?
- What admin tasks happen before and after each appointment?
- How are notes, letters, reports, or follow-up instructions created?
- What information can be safely standardised without touching clinical judgement?
- Which compliance, privacy, or consent requirements affect workflow design?
- How do practitioners find policies, templates, forms, or service information?
- What reporting is required for funding bodies, insurers, management, or compliance?

## Trades, Field Services, and Construction

Use for electricians, plumbers, builders, maintenance businesses, cleaning companies, landscapers, installers, and field teams.

- How do job requests come in?
- How are jobs triaged, quoted, scheduled, and dispatched?
- What information is often missing before a quote or site visit?
- How are photos, notes, measurements, and job details captured?
- What happens after a technician or tradesperson completes a job?
- How are invoices, variations, purchase orders, or approvals created?
- Where does scheduling break down?
- How do customers receive updates?
- What questions do customers ask repeatedly before or after a job?
- Which supplier, inventory, or parts workflows are manual?
- What safety, compliance, or certification records must be kept?
- Which weekly reports or job profitability checks are manual?

## Retail and Ecommerce

Use for online stores, Amazon/eBay sellers, boutiques, wholesalers, product brands, and inventory-led businesses.

- Where do sales come from?
- Which channels matter most: website, marketplace, social, wholesale, retail, or ads?
- How do customer questions arrive?
- What questions are asked repeatedly about products, shipping, returns, stock, or sizing?
- How are orders, fulfilment, returns, and refunds handled?
- Where does inventory data live?
- How are suppliers found, evaluated, contacted, and followed up?
- What reporting is done for ads, sales, stock, margin, or forecasting?
- Which product descriptions, listings, emails, or marketing assets are created manually?
- How do you decide what to reorder?
- What causes delays or mistakes in fulfilment?
- Where are reviews, complaints, or support tickets tracked?

## Hospitality, Events, and Venues

Use for restaurants, cafes, hotels, event venues, caterers, tourism operators, and experience businesses.

- How do bookings or enquiries arrive?
- What questions do customers ask before booking?
- How quickly can the team respond when staff are busy on-site?
- What information must be collected before confirming a booking or event?
- How are quotes, packages, menus, run sheets, or event details created?
- Which follow-ups happen before and after a booking?
- What customer updates are repeated?
- How are staff rosters, suppliers, inventory, or prep tasks managed?
- What reports are manually prepared for marketing, bookings, occupancy, sales, or management?
- What would one extra booking or event per month be worth?
- Where does service quality depend on one experienced staff member?
- What seasonal peaks create the most admin pressure?

## Education and Training

Use for schools, training providers, tutoring businesses, RTOs, academies, workshops, and course creators.

- How do student, parent, or enrolment enquiries arrive?
- What information is needed before enrolment?
- How are schedules, classes, trainers, rooms, or resources managed?
- What questions do students or parents ask repeatedly?
- How is feedback recorded and shared?
- How are assessments, attendance, certificates, or progress updates managed?
- What content, lesson material, emails, or learning resources are created manually?
- Where do trainers or instructors repeat the same explanations?
- How are compliance, accreditation, or reporting requirements handled?
- Which onboarding steps are repeated for each student or cohort?
- Where does student support fall behind?
- What reporting is needed for management, regulators, parents, or funders?

## Financial Services and Insurance

Use for brokers, planners, insurance agencies, finance teams, lenders, and advisory businesses. Avoid regulated advice during intake.

- How do new client or policy enquiries arrive?
- What information is collected before a recommendation, quote, or application?
- Which documents are requested repeatedly?
- Where do applications or renewals get delayed?
- What questions do clients ask repeatedly about status, documents, premiums, loans, claims, or next steps?
- How are compliance notes, file notes, and audit trails maintained?
- What reminders or review processes are manual?
- How are handoffs managed between adviser, admin, lender, insurer, or client?
- Which reports are prepared manually?
- What client communication needs to be consistent but personalised?
- What data cannot be entered into third-party AI tools?
- What work uses senior adviser time unnecessarily?

## Legal and Compliance-Heavy Businesses

Use for law firms, migration agents, compliance consultants, safety consultants, and regulated service providers. Avoid legal advice during intake.

- How do new matters or client requests arrive?
- How are enquiries screened and triaged?
- What documents are collected at intake?
- Which templates, precedents, or checklists are used repeatedly?
- What questions do clients ask repeatedly about process, status, or documents?
- Where do matters stall waiting for client information?
- How are deadlines, obligations, or follow-ups tracked?
- How are file notes and matter summaries created?
- Which parts of client communication can be standardised without giving advice?
- What knowledge base would help junior staff find internal guidance?
- What privacy, confidentiality, or professional obligations affect AI use?
- Which reports are needed for partners, clients, or regulators?

## Manufacturing, Wholesale, and Logistics

Use for manufacturers, distributors, warehouses, importers, exporters, and logistics businesses.

- How do orders, purchase requests, or production jobs arrive?
- Where does order information get re-entered manually?
- How are stock levels, backorders, supplier delays, and fulfilment tracked?
- What customer questions are repeated about availability, delivery, pricing, or order status?
- How are quotes, invoices, pick lists, packing slips, or delivery documents created?
- Where do errors happen between sales, warehouse, finance, and delivery?
- How are supplier follow-ups handled?
- What reports are prepared for stock, production, sales, margin, or delivery performance?
- What forecasting or reorder decisions rely on spreadsheets?
- Which processes are still paper-based?
- What approvals are required for purchasing, production, or dispatch?
- Which delays are most costly?

## Nonprofits, Associations, and Community Organisations

Use for charities, member organisations, clubs, associations, foundations, and community service providers.

- Who does the organisation serve?
- How do donors, members, volunteers, participants, or stakeholders contact you?
- What admin work is repeated around events, services, donations, membership, or reporting?
- How are volunteers, rosters, communications, and follow-ups managed?
- What questions do members, donors, or participants ask repeatedly?
- How are grants, impact reports, board reports, or funding updates prepared?
- Where is important program or service knowledge documented?
- Which tasks are delayed because staff are stretched?
- How are privacy, consent, safeguarding, or funding obligations handled?
- What reporting would save the team the most time?
- Which workflows create the most stress during busy periods?
- Where could automation improve service without making the experience impersonal?

## Scoring Hints for Report Analysis

Use these signals after the call to prioritise recommendations.

### Strong Quick-Win Signals

- Repeated weekly task with clear steps.
- High-volume customer or staff questions.
- Manual reporting from known systems.
- Lead response delay with known customer value.
- Copying data between common software tools.
- Existing documents suitable for a knowledge assistant.
- Owner spends time on low-judgement admin.

### Caution Signals

- Workflow is not understood clearly.
- Process is broken and needs redesign before automation.
- Data is sensitive or regulated.
- Tools are outdated or inaccessible.
- Team is unlikely to adopt a new system.
- Owner wants a complex custom build before proving the quick win.
- The value cannot be quantified at all.

### Impact Categories

- Owner time saved.
- Staff admin reduced.
- Faster customer response.
- More leads converted.
- Fewer errors or missed steps.
- Better reporting visibility.
- Better staff consistency and training.

### Implementation Categories

- Process optimisation.
- Zapier or Make automation.
- CRM or booking workflow improvement.
- Reporting dashboard.
- Custom GPT or knowledge assistant.
- Voice or chat intake agent.
- Speed-to-lead response agent.
- Document or email drafting workflow.
